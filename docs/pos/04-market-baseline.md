# POS Market Baseline (Feature Expectations)

Sources and takeaways:

- https://www.shopify.com/pos/features
  - Baseline POS capabilities commonly include inventory management, staff roles, checkout flows (returns, draft orders), customer profiles, and reporting/analytics.
  - Multi-location inventory and barcode-based workflows are standard for retail POS.

- https://help.shopify.com/en/manual/your-account/users/roles/permissions/pos-permissions
  - POS systems typically expose granular role permissions, including analytics visibility and register/cash tracking controls.

- https://help.shopify.com/en/manual/sell-in-person/shopify-pos/cash-register-management/cash-tracking
  - Cash tracking is modeled as register sessions with opening/closing cash counts and discrepancy review.

- https://squareup.com/au/en/point-of-sale/features
  - Expected checkout features include split tender, custom tender types, receipts, automatic discounts, and optional offline payments.

- https://squareup.com/help/us/en/article/5097-process-split-payments
  - Split payments are a standard POS flow and typically require the balance to be fully settled before completion.

- https://www.lightspeedhq.com/pos/retail/features/
  - Multi-location inventory, variants, stock transfers, barcode labels, and purchase-order workflows are common POS expectations for retail.

- https://technologyadvice.com/blog/sales/pos-features/
  - The commonly cited POS feature baseline includes inventory management, customer profiles, checkout/payment, security, reporting, mobility, marketing/loyalty, omnichannel selling, staff management, multi-store operations, advanced inventory/vendor management, shipping/delivery, kitchen displays, work orders, appointments, subscriptions, and ID checks.
